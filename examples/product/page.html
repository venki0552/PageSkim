Overview

The Aurora K2 is a 75% wireless mechanical keyboard built around a CNC-milled aluminium case and a gasket-mounted polycarbonate plate. It connects over 2.4 GHz (included dongle), Bluetooth 5.3 to three paired devices, or USB-C, and switches between them with a dedicated key. The hot-swap PCB accepts any 3- or 5-pin MX-style switch, so the stock switches are a starting point, not a commitment.

Battery and connectivity

A 4000 mAh battery runs the K2 for up to 320 hours with the backlight off, or around 40 hours at full brightness. Charging over USB-C takes 2.5 hours and the keyboard works while charging. On 2.4 GHz the polling rate is 1000 Hz — the same as wired — while Bluetooth polls at 125 Hz, which we recommend for typing rather than gaming.

Frequently asked questions

Does the K2 work with macOS, Windows and Linux?

Yes. A physical toggle switches the modifier layout between Mac and Windows/Linux legends; both sets of keycaps are included. Firmware updates ship for all three platforms.

Can I remap keys?

The K2 runs QMK-compatible firmware with VIA support, so every key and layer is remappable in the browser with no software install.

Is there a low-profile version?

Not yet. A low-profile K2 LP is on the public roadmap for late 2026.
